Australia's Role in Global Fossil Fuel Polution & Climate Change

This article aims explores domestic fossil fuel consumption and exports along with their overall effects on climate change and CO₂ emissions and Australia's ultimate role in the pollution caused by fossil fuel usage to global warming.

Introduction

Australia plays a major role globally in the fossil fuels market as the world's 3rd largest exporter, making up 7% of global fossil fuel exports. Australia is the 5th largest miner of fossil fuel carbon, and sits at ten times the world average per capita. Additionally, the country's over-reliance on fossil fuels for energy means its domestice greenhouse gas emissions are at 14th globally and in the top 10 per capita (Swann, 2019). Whilst on paper Australia may make up only a small proportion of global emissions, its role in supplying fossil fuels globally and high per capita usage make its overall contribution to global warming and climate change a serious concern.

Coal & LNG Summary Statistics

Autralia's greatest fossil fuel production and exports are black coal and natural gas. From 2022-2023, coal made up a total of $113.8b aud in export income whilst gas made up a further $90.3b (AECR, 2024).

Key Takeaway

Australia's fossil fuel exports are composed mainly of black coal and liquified natural gas (LNG) which contribute significantly to the economy (AECR, 2024). As the world shifts towards more sustainable energy sources to combat climate change, it is important to evaluate Australia's position as both a major exporter and contributor to greenhouse gas emissions in its quest for economic growth and question its environmental responsibilities.
